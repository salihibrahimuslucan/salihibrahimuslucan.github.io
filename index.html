Native · Protocols

AquaCONFIG v2 — Native DMX/RDM Device Address Manager

Problem

Commissioning lighting and fountain installs means discovering, addressing, and configuring DMX/RDM fixtures in the field — and off-the-shelf tools couldn't expose everything the team needed.

What I built

A dependency-free native Windows desktop app implementing the full ANSI E1.20 (RDM) stack on DMX512 — device discovery, standard GET/SET messages, and custom manufacturer-specific PIDs. While testing against real hardware I root-caused a fault in a PowerBox repeater that was silently blocking RDM traffic.

Stack
C++Win32 APIGDI DMX512ANSI E1.20 RDMCustom PIDs USB-to-DMX serial
Outcome

A lightweight on-site tool for addressing and troubleshooting fixtures. The RDM stack handles standard and custom parameters, and the PowerBox diagnosis unblocked commissioning — a root-cause find at the protocol/hardware boundary.

Automation · CAD

Electrical Control-Panel CAD Automation Engine

Problem

Every fountain project needs its own electrical control-panel schematics (DWG). Drawing them by hand is repetitive, slow, inconsistent across projects, and easy to get subtly wrong.

What I built

A Python automation engine that generates manufacturing-ready schematics from a structured component netlist — modelling components, nets, and the BOM, running electrical-rule checks (ERC), and emitting deterministic DWG/DXF. Headless via ezdxf, with AutoCAD for conversion, parametric primitives, a TDD regression suite, and a lint gate that blocks invalid drawings.

Stack
PythonezdxfAutoCAD / accoreconsole DXF / DWGnetlist / ERC / BOM TDD (pytest)Git
Outcome

Consistent, rule-checked panel drawings straight from project data, replacing manual drafting. Deterministic output means drawings are reproducible, diffable, and reviewable — and the lint gate stops malformed schematics before the shop floor.

Data · Pipeline

Aquatronic CRM Master Database — Lead Consolidation & Scoring

Problem

Years of trade-fair contacts were scattered across inconsistent sources — exports, spreadsheets, notes — with duplicates, fabricated entries, and no way to prioritize follow-up. Effectively unusable for sales.

What I built

I consolidated thousands of trade-fair contacts into one master database, then designed a HOT / HIGH / MEDIUM / LOW priority-scoring system. I ran a structured, multi-pass data-quality cleanup — deduplication, field validation, and systematic removal of fabricated records — as a repeatable process, not a one-off.

Stack
Python data pipelineRule-based lead scoring Spreadsheet integration
Outcome

A single, deduplicated, prioritized contact base the sales team can actually work from, with a defined scoring tier so the highest-value leads surface first.

Automation · OCR

Business-Card / Fair-Notebook OCR Scanner

Problem

Staff return from trade fairs with handwritten notebooks and stacks of business cards. Re-typing all of it into the CRM by hand is slow and error-prone.

What I built

A Flask web tool that ingests scanned pages and PDFs, extracts text with pdfplumber, runs Tesseract OCR on images and handwritten notes, and outputs structured contact data ready to feed the CRM pipeline.

Stack
PythonFlask pdfplumberTesseract OCR
Outcome

Turns handwritten fair notebooks into structured digital contacts, removing manual data entry and feeding directly into the CRM consolidation workflow.
